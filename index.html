THE BIG PICTURE No jargon, just the idea

A trusted directory for the world's AI agents.

Soon, billions of AI assistants will do things for us: booking trips, handling support, negotiating, and talking to each other. For that to be safe, they need a reliable way to find each other and prove who they are. The NANDA Index is that directory: a public, tamper-proof address book for AI agents.

Why it matters

The internet's address book is outdated

The internet already has an address book. It turns a name you type, like a website, into the place it actually lives. The problem is that it was built decades ago for websites that rarely change, not for AI agents that appear, move and disappear constantly. Five things start to break.

01

Too many, too fast

There could be billions of agents, popping up and moving every few seconds. The old system simply was not designed to keep up.

02

No way to know who is real

A web address proves a name, not that the agent behind it is honest or can actually do what it claims. We need real proof.

03

Your lookups are exposed

Today, looking something up can reveal who is asking and what they want. Companies and people need that to stay private.

04

Always reachable

Agents move around to handle load and stay online. You still need to reach the right one, right now, wherever it is.

05

Accountability

If an agent misbehaves, there should be a clear, tamper-proof record so people can tell what happened and who was responsible.

THE IDEA

One shared directory

Fix all of this with a single, trusted directory built for agents, without tearing down the internet we already have.

What it is

A verified ID that points to live details

Think of it like a verified business listing. Each agent gets a tiny ID card that cannot be faked. The card does not hold much itself; it points to a live page that the agent keeps up to date with what it can do and how to reach it. The directory makes five promises.

1

Works with every kind of agent

Agents built for NANDA and agents already listed somewhere else can all be found in one place.

2

Instantly findable

A brand-new agent can be found anywhere in the world in under a second.

3

Trust can be pulled instantly

If an agent is hacked or goes rogue, its verified status can be removed in under a second.

4

Claims are checked, not just stated

An agent cannot simply say what it can do. Its abilities are verified and signed by a trusted source.

5

Private by design

You can look up an agent without revealing who you are or what you are after.

One directory, many kinds of agents. NANDA does not try to control every agent. Companies, governments and individuals decide what to list; the directory just points you to the right place, like a switchboard.

How it works

Three simple parts

You do not need to understand the plumbing, but here is the whole idea in three parts.

PART 1

A tiny verified ID

Each agent gets a small ID card with a tamper-proof seal, like a passport stamp. It is too small to fake and quick to check.

PART 2

A live details page

The agent keeps its own up-to-date page of what it does and where to reach it. The ID card just points to it, so details can change without re-issuing the card.

PART 3

Smart routing

When you call an agent, the directory sends you to the right working address, even as the agent moves around to stay fast and online.
